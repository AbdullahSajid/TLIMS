Frequently Asked Questions FAQs about TLIMS

What are the missions of developing TLIMS?

TLIMS is a web application, developed for National Textile Research Center (NTRC). It's a private software. It's not in the public domain. Only NTRC has a proprietary right to access this software. But NTRC and IT Staff cannot sale or distribute this application without the consent of developers. It is developed to ease the management of textile samples testing in labs of NTRC. It covers the procedure of labs and provide the functionality of managing all the common tasks in labs. For more information about NTRC and its services, follow the links below

  • National Textile University
  • Textile Testing and Product Development Services

What are the access privileges of admin and other users of TLIMS?

As there are three types of users, so there are three types of access privileges.

  • Admin
    • Admin has complete power and is a power distribution source. As a root manager, admin can create, edit and delete the users of this system. Admin has global privileges. Admin can manage the users and can examine the status of tests and he/she has the right to finalize and generate reports and checking statistics
  • Labs Managers
    • Lab Managers can create, edit, delete the test result reports and is capable of managing all the tasks related to lab. He/She has limited privileges as compared to the admin.
  • Receptionist
    • Receptionist is responsible for interaction with the customer, receive samples and feed into the system. He/She can send email and sms notifications to the customer about the sample submission and expected delievery date information. he/She is also responsible for generating receipt to deleiver to the customer.

What is meant by sample status and how many types of status are there?

Sample Status is used to track samples at different stages of testing. There are four different stages of sample testing and each stage is represented by a different status.

Submitted Samples
When status is submitted, then it means that the receptionist has entered a new sample record but he has not confirmed yet i.e. not sent to labs for testing yet.
Pending Samples
When status is pending, then it means that the receptionist has confirmed the newly entered sample record and has delievered it to particular lab for testing.
Pending Reports
When status is completed, then it means that the lab manager has compiled all the tests of sample and has delievered the test results to receptionist.
Completed Reports
When status is finished, then it means that the receptionist has confirmed (or generated if needed) the report and has distributed it to customer and has received all pending payment.
